What is Critical Illness Insurance?

Critical Illness (CI) insurance is a robust "Living Benefit". If you are diagnosed with a covered severe medical condition (such as cancer, heart attack, or stroke) and survive the standard survival period, you receive a direct, tax-free lump sum benefit.

You Decide How to Use the Funds:

Whether traveling out-of-country for elite procedures, paying for private target drugs, settling home mortgages, or offsetting wage losses, the capital is 100% under your control.

Return of Premium (ROP)

A highly robust "win-win" option. If you remain fully healthy and never submit a claim, you can cancel the policy at specific milestones (e.g. after 15 years or at age 65) to **reclaim 100% of all paid premiums**.

Equivalent to securing free medical protection while preserving all your capital intact.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: If I make an early-stage claim, do I lose my ROP rights?

A: No. Partial claims (e.g. early-stage cancer) typically do not impact your ultimate right to reclaim 100% of your paid premiums upon maturity.

Q: Should I secure Term Life or Critical Illness?

A: They serve separate goals. Term Life protects your family upon your passing; Critical Illness protects your personal finance while you survive severe illness. A comprehensive plan blends both.
