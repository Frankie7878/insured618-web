Taxation Challenges

Why Do Corporate Owners Need Specialized Planning?

The Challenge: Following tax rule updates, excessive passive corporate investment income (e.g., rental yields, interest) will erode your Small Business Deduction (SBD) threshold, subjecting your active business to significantly higher tax brackets.

The Opportunity: Life insurance is one of the very few vehicles recognized by the CRA that offers tax-deferred growth within the corporation, and allows the final death benefit to enter the Capital Dividend Account (CDA) tax-free for distribution to shareholders.

Core Protection & Risk Management

Protecting your enterprise from sudden disruptions and ensuring seamless business continuity.

🔑

Key Person Protection

If an essential employee or shareholder falls critically ill or passes away, the payout helps offset revenue losses, repay business loans, and fund recruitments to reassure creditors and clients.

🤝

Buy-Sell Agreement Funding

Use tax-free insurance benefits to purchase the shares of a deceased partner. This preserves business control for active partners while providing fair, immediate cash liquidation to their family.

🛡️

Commercial Loan Protection

Use term life or critical illness insurance as collateral for commercial bank loans. This ensures that any business liabilities will not overflow to drag down your family or assets.

Advanced Tax & Wealth Transfer Strategies

Maximize your wealth by converting taxable corporate surplus into highly liquid tax-free assets.

Foundation

A. Corporate Asset Transfer

Reallocate highly taxed corporate passive investments (e.g. cash, GICs) into a participating permanent life policy.

  • ✓ Tax-deferred Growth: Cash value accumulates within the policy completely tax-free.
  • ✓ CDA Efficiency: Death benefit transfers to shareholder beneficiaries tax-free through the CDA, bypassing massive inheritance taxes.
Advancement

B. Corporate Retirement Strategy

Fund corporate policies to build a stable asset class that can be collateralized at retirement to provide a tax-free cash stream.

  • ✓ Higher Net Payout: Outperforms traditional corporate dividend extraction routes.
  • ✓ Retain Security: The death benefit remains active as a powerful wealth legacy buffer.
HNW First Choice
High Net Worth

C. Immediate Financing Arrangement (IFA)

Obtain a large permanent policy and immediately secure a bank credit line against it to inject cash back into active business investments.

  • ✓ Double Asset Leverage: Acquire robust death benefits while retaining maximum working capital liquidity.
  • ✓ Tax Deductions: Loan interest may be eligible as a tax-deductible business expense.

Shareholder & Executive Benefits

Shared Ownership Strategy

The corporation and a key executive split policy ownership. The company pays for the death benefit, while the executive owns and pays for the tax-deferred cash accumulator. A powerful, tax-efficient retention tool.

Corporate Critical Illness ROP

The company pays for the critical illness policy. If the business owner stays healthy and retires, the refund of premium (ROP) can be paid back to the business owner, serving as a highly tax-efficient surplus extraction tool.

📂 Success Case: Anthony's Story

Scenario: Anthony, age 45, is an established business owner.

Challenge: Worried that a severe medical issue will stall his business income and severely drain his hard-earned retirement assets.

Solution: Acquired a corporate-owned Critical Illness policy with a Return of Premium (ROP) rider.

Outcome:
  • If diagnosed: Receives a tax-free lump-sum payout to keep business operating and fund premium out-of-province medical trials.
  • If healthy at retirement: Reclaims 100% of all paid corporate premiums to fund his retirement, completely preserving corporate capital.
